# Architectural Styles

A reference catalog of architectural styles and building types for world builders. Pick a style, apply it to a zone, and the buildings in that zone follow its visual language.

---

## 1. Historical Styles

### Ancient/Classical

| Style | Period | Key Features |
|-------|--------|--------------|
| Egyptian | 3000-300 BC | Massive stone blocks, flat roofs, pylons, obelisks, hieroglyphs, papyrus columns, symmetry, limestone/sandstone, tomb pyramids, sphinx statues |
| Sumerian/Mesopotamian | 4500-500 BC | Ziggurats (stepped mud-brick temples), thick clay walls, cuneiform inscriptions, arched gateways, courtyard houses, canals, glazed brick reliefs |
| Greek | 800-100 BC | Marble columns (Doric/Ionic/Corinthian), pediments, open-air temples, agora marketplaces, amphitheaters, tiled roofs, whitewashed stone, symmetry |
| Roman | 500 BC-500 AD | Arches, domes, concrete, aqueducts, colosseum-style arenas, forums, baths, mosaics, columns, vaults, roads, insulae apartment blocks |
| Persian | 550 BC-650 AD | Columned halls (apadana), paradise gardens, windcatchers, iwans (arched portals), blue tilework, barrel vaults, fire temples, rock-cut tombs |
| Mesoamerican | 2000 BC-1500 AD | Step pyramids, temple platforms, ball courts, carved stone facades, jaguar/serpent motifs, plazas, astronomical alignment, stucco painted walls |
| Minoan | 2700-1450 BC | Multi-story palaces, light wells, grand staircases, bull-horn symbols, colorful frescoes, column-lined halls, drainage systems, central courtyards |
| Mycenaean | 1600-1100 BC | Cyclopean stone walls, beehive tombs (tholoi), Lion Gate style entrances, megaron halls with central hearth, fortified hilltop citadels |
| Phoenician/Carthaginian | 1500-146 BC | Ashlar stone masonry, harbor-centered cities, tophet sanctuaries, purple-dyed sails on quays, columned temples blending Egyptian and Greek forms |
| Etruscan | 900-100 BC | Tumulus tombs, terracotta roof decorations, arched city gates, necropolis cities of the dead, frescoed burial chambers |
| Viking/Norse | 800-1100 AD | Longhouses, stave churches, dragon-head carvings, turf roofs, timber frame, great halls with central hearth, shield walls, carved doorways |
| Celtic | 800 BC-400 AD | Round stone houses, hill forts with concentric earthworks, crannog lake dwellings, interlaced knotwork carvings, dry stone walls |

### Medieval

| Style | Period | Key Features |
|-------|--------|--------------|
| Romanesque | 900-1200 | Thick walls, round arches, barrel vaults, fortress-like churches, small windows, towers, stone construction, heavy and solid |
| Gothic | 1100-1500 | Pointed arches, flying buttresses, ribbed vaults, stained glass, gargoyles, rose windows, tall spires, stone tracery, cathedrals reaching skyward |
| Tudor | 1485-1603 | Half-timbered walls (black beams + white plaster), steep gabled roofs, tall chimneys, leaded glass windows, brick lower floors, asymmetric |
| Byzantine | 330-1453 | Domed roofs (pendentive domes), mosaics covering interiors, gold backgrounds, round arches, brick/stone, central plan churches, ornate decoration |
| Moorish/Islamic | 700-1500 | Horseshoe arches, geometric tile patterns (zellige), muqarnas (honeycomb vaults), minarets, courtyards with fountains, mashrabiya screens, calligraphy |
| Norman | 1066-1200 | Massive square keeps, round arches, thick walls, motte-and-bailey castles, chevron/zigzag carved decoration, barrel-vaulted undercrofts |
| Carolingian | 750-900 | Westwork (monumental western entrance), basilica plan, palatine chapels, gatehouse complexes, revived Roman forms, heavy masonry |
| Timber Frame (Fachwerk) | 1200-1700 | Exposed timber skeleton filled with wattle-and-daub or brick, each story may overhang (jettying), carved corner posts, decorative panels |
| Hanseatic | 1200-1500 | Stepped brick gables, tall narrow warehouses (kontors), red brick, multi-story storage with crane beams, merchant guild halls |
| Castle/Fortress | 1000-1500 | Curtain walls, towers, battlements (crenellations), moats, drawbridge, keep, barbican, murder holes, arrow slits, portcullis, great hall |

### Renaissance to Industrial

| Style | Period | Key Features |
|-------|--------|--------------|
| Renaissance | 1400-1600 | Symmetry, proportion, columns (classical orders), domes, rusticated stone, arcades, loggias, pediments, palazzo style, humanism |
| Baroque | 1600-1750 | Grandeur, curves, gilt, dramatic lighting, oval shapes, large-scale frescoes, twisted columns (Solomonic), elaborate ornamentation, palaces |
| Rococo | 1720-1780 | Light pastels, asymmetric ornament, shell and scroll motifs, gilded plasterwork, intimate salon rooms, playful curves, mirrors |
| Colonial | 1600-1800 | Symmetrical facade, central door, shuttered windows, dormers, brick or clapboard, front porch, modest ornamentation, chimneys |
| Georgian | 1714-1830 | Symmetry, sash windows, brick, classical door surrounds, fan lights, hipped roofs, iron railings, terraced housing |
| Neoclassical | 1750-1850 | Return to Greek/Roman forms, monumental columns, triangular pediments, clean lines, restrained decoration, government buildings |
| Victorian | 1837-1901 | Ornate decoration, bay windows, turrets, wrap-around porches, patterned shingles, iron cresting, steeply pitched roofs, gingerbread trim |
| Gothic Revival | 1740-1900 | Pointed arches revived in modern materials, pinnacles, tracery, polychrome brick, church and university buildings |
| Art Nouveau | 1890-1910 | Organic flowing lines, plant-inspired forms, curved glass, ironwork, asymmetry, whiplash curves, stained glass, nature motifs |
| Art Deco | 1920-1940 | Geometric patterns, stepped forms, chrome/steel, sunburst motifs, zigzag patterns, bold colors, streamlined shapes, skyscrapers |
| Industrial | 1800-present | Exposed brick, steel beams, large windows, open floor plans, high ceilings, factory aesthetic, pipes visible, concrete, utilitarian |
| Beaux-Arts | 1880-1920 | Classical grandeur, paired columns, elaborate sculpture, cartouches, balustrades, grand staircases, mansard roofs, monumental scale |

| Style | Region | Key Features |
|-------|--------|--------------|
| Chinese Traditional | China | Curved roofs (upswept eaves), red/gold colors, wooden framework (dougong brackets), symmetry, courtyards (siheyuan), dragon/phoenix motifs, pagodas, moon gates |
| Chinese Imperial | China | Forbidden City style, yellow glazed tiles (imperial only), vermilion walls, marble terraces, carved balustrades, spirit walls, nine-dragon screens |
| Japanese Traditional | Japan | Sliding screens (shoji/fusuma), tatami floors, engawa (veranda), minimalism, natural materials, curved roofs, zen gardens, torii gates, tea houses |
| Japanese Castle | Japan | Stone foundations with curved walls (batter), white plaster walls, multi-tiered towers (tenshu), loopholes, complex gate systems, decorative roof tiles |
| Korean Traditional | Korea | Hanok houses, ondol heated floors, curved tile roofs, wooden columns, stone foundations, dancheong (painted bracket patterns), balanced with nature |
| Tibetan | Tibet | Thick inward-sloping stone/mud walls, flat roofs, small deeply-set windows, whitewashed walls with dark borders, fortress-monasteries (dzongs) |
| Mongolian | Mongolia | Ger (yurt) felt tents, circular frame, lattice walls, toono (roof ring), portable, decorated entrance doors, central hearth with chimney |

| Buddhist | Various | Stupas (domed shrines), prayer wheel walls, monastery courtyards, chaitya halls (arched prayer halls), carved cave temples |

### African

| Style | Region | Key Features |
|-------|--------|--------------|
| Sudano-Sahelian | Mali/Ghana/Niger | Mud-brick (banco), flat roofs, buttressed walls, protruding wooden beams (toron), pinnacles, organic rounded forms, thick insulating walls |
| Aksumite/Ethiopian | Ethiopia | Rock-hewn churches (Lalibela), thatched tukul round houses, stone stelae (obelisks), castle-like Gonderene architecture, stepped stone platforms |
| North African/Maghreb | Morocco/Tunisia | Riads (courtyard houses), zellige tilework, carved stucco (tadelakt walls), flat roofs with terraces, kasbahs (fortified villages), narrow medina streets |
| Great Zimbabwe | Southern Africa | Dry stone walls without mortar (coursed granite), circular enclosures (imba huru), conical towers, chevron wall decorations |
| Swahili Coast | East Africa | Coral stone houses, carved wooden doors, inner courtyards, flat roofs, plaster decoration, narrow streets, integration of Arabic and African forms |
| Zulu/Southern Bantu | Southern Africa | Beehive thatched huts (indlu), circular kraal compounds, cattle enclosure at center, woven grass and saplings, low doorways |
| Dogon | Mali | Cliff-face settlements, granary towers with thatched caps, toguna (meeting shelter with thick layered roof), adobe and stone mix |

### Pre-Columbian Americas

| Style | Region | Key Features |
|-------|--------|--------------|
| Inca | Andes | Precisely fitted stone (no mortar), trapezoidal doors/windows, terraced construction, stone water channels, fortress walls (Sacsayhuaman style) |
| Maya | Central America | Pyramid temples with roof combs, corbel arch vaults, carved stelae, elaborate stucco facades, sacbe (raised roads), astronomical observatories |
| Aztec | Central Mexico | Twin temple pyramids (Templo Mayor style), skull racks (tzompantli), eagle/jaguar warrior motifs, carved stone, chinampas (floating gardens) |
| Pueblo | Southwest US | Adobe mud-brick, multi-story stepped structures, flat roofs, ladders between levels, rounded edges, earth-tone colors, kiva (underground ceremonial room) |
| Northwest Coast | Pacific NW | Massive cedar longhouses, totem poles, carved post and beam, plank construction, painted facades depicting clan crests |
| Mississippian | Eastern US | Flat-topped earthen mounds, palisaded towns, wattle-and-daub houses, central plaza, charnel houses on mound summits |

### Middle Eastern and Central Asian

| Style | Region | Key Features |
|-------|--------|--------------|
| Ottoman | Turkey | Grand mosques with cascading domes, pencil minarets, ablution fountains, hammams (bathhouses), wooden houses with projecting upper floors |

### Oceanian and Pacific
